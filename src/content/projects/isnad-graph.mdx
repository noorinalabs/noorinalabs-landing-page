## Mapping the Chains of Transmission

The Isnad Graph brings computational analysis to one of the oldest systems of
knowledge verification in history. Every hadith — a report of the words or
actions of the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) — is accompanied by an
_isnad_, a chain of narrators stretching back to an eyewitness.

Classical hadith scholars spent lifetimes cataloguing these chains, evaluating
narrators, and tracing connections. The Isnad Graph makes that centuries-old
scholarly tradition navigable, searchable, and visual.

## How It Works

The platform ingests narrator and chain data from published scholarly sources,
structures it as a graph database, and presents it through an interactive web
interface. Researchers, students, and curious learners can explore the network
of transmission that underpins the hadith corpus.

## Who It's For

- **Researchers** studying hadith sciences (_mustalah al-hadith_) and narrator
  criticism (_ilm al-rijal_)
- **Students** seeking an intuitive way to understand chains of narration
- **Anyone** curious about the scholarly tradition behind hadith authentication
